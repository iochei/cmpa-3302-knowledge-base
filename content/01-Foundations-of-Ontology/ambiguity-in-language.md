Human language is inherently ambiguous. This presents one of the biggest challenges in Information Architecture. A single word can have multiple meanings, and multiple words can describe the same concept (synonyms).

### The Problem of Meaning

Consider the word "Bank". Without context, it is ambiguous:
*   Is it the side of a river? (Financial context)
*   Is it a financial institution where money is kept? (Geographical context)
*   Is it the action of tilting an aircraft? (Aviation context)

If your IA uses the label "Bank" on a navigation menu without clear context, users will be confused.

### IA's Role in Reducing Ambiguity

Information Architects must identify and manage this ambiguity through:
*   **Context:** Providing surrounding information to clarify labels.
*   **Controlled Vocabularies:** Standardizing the terms used within the system.
*   **Synonym Management:** Ensuring users find content regardless of the search term they use.

Good IA doesn't eliminate ambiguity; it manages and clarifies it for the user.
