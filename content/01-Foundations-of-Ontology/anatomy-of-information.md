In Information Architecture, it is crucial to understand the hierarchy of how raw data is transformed into useful knowledge. This is often visualized as the DIKW Pyramid (Data, Information, Knowledge, Wisdom).

### The DIKW Hierarchy

1.  **Data:** Raw, unorganized facts and symbols. (e.g., the numbers "34", "98.6", "Cloudy"). Data lacks context.
2.  **Information:** Data that has been processed, organized, and given context. (e.g., "It is 98.6 degrees Fahrenheit in Dallas today."). Data made meaningful.
3.  **Knowledge:** Information that has been synthesized, understood, and applied by a human. (e.g., "It is hot in Dallas today, so I should wear light clothing."). Information integrated with experience.
4.  **Wisdom:** The judicious application of knowledge to solve complex problems or make decisions. (e.g., "Understanding long-term climate trends in Dallas based on accumulated temperature data.").

### Why This Matters to IA

As architects of information, our job is to elevate raw **data** into accessible **information** so that users can build **knowledge**.
