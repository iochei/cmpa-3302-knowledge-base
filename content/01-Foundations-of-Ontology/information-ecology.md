Information Architecture does not exist in a vacuum. It is part of a complex **Information Ecology**. Coined by Bonnie Nardi and Vicki O'Day, this concept adapts the biological idea of an ecosystem to information environments, emphasizing the interconnected relationships between people, practices, values, and technologies in a particular setting.

### The Three Components of the Ecology

1.  **Content:** What the system contains (documents, data, media, applications).
2.  **Context:** The environment, goals, politics, and culture of the organization and its users.
3.  **Users:** The people who interact with the system, their needs, and their behaviors.

### Why This Matters to IA

A successful Information Architecture must account for all three elements:

*   You cannot organize **Content** without understanding the **Context** in which it was created.
*   You cannot design for **Users** without understanding the **Content** they need to access.
*   The **Context** defines the constraints and goals for both the **Content** and the **Users**.

***

> "An information ecology is a system of people, practices, values, and technologies in a particular local environment." — Nardi & Day
