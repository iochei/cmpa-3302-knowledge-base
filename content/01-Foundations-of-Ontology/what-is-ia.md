Information Architecture (IA) is the art and science of organizing and structuring content and data effectively. The primary goal of IA is to help users find information and complete tasks.

It bridges the gap between where the user is (the "mess") and where they want to be (the information).

### Key Components of IA

*   **Ontology:** The meaning and categorization of information (what things are called).
*   **Taxonomy:** How information is structured and related (the hierarchy).
*   **Choreography:** How users move through and interact with the information.

Without a solid IA, digital systems become difficult to navigate, leading to user frustration and abandonment.

***

> "Information Architecture is the structural design of shared information environments." — Louis Rosenfeld and Peter Morville
