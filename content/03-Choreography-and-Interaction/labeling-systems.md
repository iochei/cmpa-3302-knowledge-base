Labeling systems are the most visible part of an Information Architecture. They represent the terms, icons, and links used in your navigation, menus, and buttons. A good labeling system is essential for effective [Wayfinding Principles](03-Choreography-and-Interaction/wayfinding.md).

### The Challenge of Labels

Labels are inherently tied to [Ambiguity in Language](01-Foundations-of-Ontology/ambiguity-in-language.md). A single word can mean different things to different users. The goal of the IA is to select labels that are:

*   **Clear and Unambiguous:** Users should immediately understand what content lies behind the link. (e.g., "Support" is better than "Assistance").
*   **Consistent:** Use the same term for the same content across the entire site. Don't call it "Services" in the top nav and "Our Work" in the footer.
*   **User-Centered:** Use the language your users use, not the internal terminology of your organization. (e.g., "How to Apply" is better than "Matriculation Procedures").

### Types of Labels

1.  **Navigational Labels:** The main links in your [Navigational Schemas](02-Taxonomy-and-Structure/navigational-schemas.md).
2.  **Indexing/Tagging Labels:** The keywords or facets used to describe content for retrieval (e.g., [Faceted Classification](02-Taxonomy-and-Structure/faceted-classification.md)).
3.  **Embedded Link Labels:** Hyperlinks within the main body text (e.g., [Contextual Navigation](02-Taxonomy-and-Structure/navigational-schemas.md#3-contextual-navigation)).
