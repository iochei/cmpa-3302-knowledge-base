Coined by Information Architect Peter Morville (adapting concepts from scent-following animals), **the Scent of Information** refers to the degree to which a user can accurately predict what they will find by clicking on a specific link or label.

### Following the Trail

Users rarely navigate randomly. They follow "scent trails." When a user is on a page, they look for cues (labels, links, images, headlines) that suggest the information they are seeking is "this way."

*   **Strong Scent:** The label perfectly matches the user's expectation. (e.g., Clicking "Shipping Rates" and finding a page with a clear table of shipping costs).
*   **Weak Scent:** The label is ambiguous or misleading. (e.g., Clicking "Our Philosophy" when you are actually looking for "Company History," and finding only a mission statement).

### IA's Role in Strengthening Scent

As Information Architects, our job is to ensure every label and link provides a strong, accurate scent. We achieve this through:

1.  **Clear [Labeling Systems](03-Choreography-and-Interaction/labeling-systems.md):** Using predictable, user-centered terminology.
2.  **Strong [Contextual Navigation](02-Taxonomy-and-Structure/navigational-schemas.md#3-contextual-navigation):** Placing links to related content where users expect them to be.
3.  **Effective [Hierarchy](02-Taxonomy-and-Structure/hierarchical-structures.md):** Ensuring the site structure is logical so that broad categories clearly lead to specific sub-pages.

If the scent is lost, the user becomes disoriented and often abandons the site.
