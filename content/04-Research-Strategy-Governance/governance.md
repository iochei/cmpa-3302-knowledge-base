Information Architectures are not "set it and forget it" systems. Information is dynamic; it rots, grows, and changes. An effective Information Architecture requires a strategy for ongoing governance and maintenance to ensure it remains useful, findable, and accurate over time.

### What is Content Governance?

Content governance refers to the policies, standards, roles, processes, and accountability structures used to manage an organization's information assets. It answers critical IA questions:

*   **Who is responsible for this content?** (Content ownership).
*   **What are the standards for new content?** (Style guides, [Labeling](03-Choreography-and-Interaction/labeling-systems.md) consistency).
*   **How is content reviewed and retired?** (Content lifecycle management).

### IA Maintenance IA

Maintenance IA is the operational work required to keep the structure healthy:
*   **Managing the Taxonomy:** Updating [Controlled Vocabularies](01-Foundations-of-Ontology/ambiguity-in-language.md#ia-role-in-reducing-ambiguity) as new topics emerge.
*   **Auditing Links:** Ensuring [Contextual Navigation](02-Taxonomy-and-Structure/navigational-schemas.md#3-contextual-navigation) and cross-links are not broken (Link rot).
*   **Monitoring Search Logs:** Analyzing user queries to see if the IA is failing to [retrieve](03-Choreography-and-Interaction/search-interfaces.md) needed information.

Without governance, the IA will inevitably degrade into a chaotic mess, destroying the [User Experience](04-Research-Strategy-Governance/ux-context.md).
