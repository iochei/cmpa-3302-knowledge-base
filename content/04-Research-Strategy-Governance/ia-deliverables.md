Information Architects use a variety of documentation to visualize, communicate, and specify the IA to stakeholders, designers, and developers. These deliverables bridge the gap between abstract [Ontology](01-Foundations-of-Ontology/what-is-ia.md#key-components-of-ia) and concrete implementation.

### Common IA Deliverables

1.  **Sitemaps (Blueprints):**
    *   **Purpose:** To visualize the site's hierarchy and [Organization Systems](02-Taxonomy-and-Structure/organization-systems.md).
    *   **Description:** A diagram showing the parent-child relationships of pages. It defines the [Top-Down](02-Taxonomy-and-Structure/hierarchical-structures.md#1-top-down-approach) structure of the site.
2.  **Content Maps:**
    *   **Purpose:** To detail the structure and metadata of individual page types.
    *   **Description:** A document (often a spreadsheet) inventorying all content elements required on a specific page (e.g., "Article Page" needs: Title, Date, Author, Body, Related Links, Tags).
3.  **Wireframes (Schematics):**
    *   **Purpose:** To visualize the page-level layout and [Navigation Schemas](02-Taxonomy-and-Structure/navigational-schemas.md).
    *   **Description:** A low-fidelity skeletal drawing of a page, showing where global nav, local nav, search bars, and content blocks will be placed.
4.  **Controlled Vocabulary Lists / Thesauri:**
    *   **Purpose:** To define the approved terminology for [Labeling](03-Choreography-and-Interaction/labeling-systems.md) and [Faceted Classification](02-Taxonomy-and-Structure/faceted-classification.md).

These deliverables are the primary output of the IA strategy phase and serve as the specification for the technical build.
