# Camera Basics

To capture fleeting, candid human moments in a busy urban environment, you must move beyond "Auto" mode and master the exposure triangle: Aperture, Shutter Speed, and ISO. These three pillars dictate not only the brightness of your image but also the narrative quality of the final shot.

## The Components

1.  **Shutter Speed:** In street photography, speed is everything. You should keep your shutter speed at or above 1/250th of a second to freeze the fast-paced motion of pedestrians and traffic. Anything slower risks unwanted motion blur unless you are intentionally panning to show speed.
2.  **Aperture:** An aperture of f/8 is widely considered the "sweet spot." It provides a deep depth of field, ensuring that most of your scene is in focus even if you do not have time to perfectly nail your focus on a moving subject.
3.  **ISO:** ISO should be your last resort. In daylight, keep it low (100–200). Only increase it when the sun goes down, accepting that a little digital noise is often better than a blurry photo.

> "Your eye must see a composition or an expression that life itself offers you, and you must know with intuition when to click the camera." — Henri Cartier-Bresson

Mastering these manual settings is about freeing your mind. When you no longer have to consciously think about settings, you can focus entirely on [[composition-rules]]. This technical foundation is what allows you to react instantly to the unpredictable nature of street life. Furthermore, these settings change drastically depending on the [[lighting-conditions]] you encounter, so practice adjusting them by feel rather than looking at your screen.
