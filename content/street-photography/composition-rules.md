# Composition Rules

Composition is what separates a mere snapshot from a powerful, intentional photograph. It is the framework through which you organize the chaos of the city.

## Key Techniques
* **Rule of Thirds:** Place your subject off-center to create balance and tension.
* **Leading Lines:** Use architecture, fences, or roads to draw the viewer’s eye directly to your subject.
* **Framing:** Use windows, doorways, or shadows as natural frames to isolate your subject within the larger urban environment.

The beauty of street photography is that these rules are often meant to be broken. However, knowing them allows you to intentionally create a sense of order. When you combine these rules with the technical knowledge from [[camera-basics]] and the situational awareness of [[lighting-conditions]], you become a much more effective photographer. Always remember to carry your [[essential-gear]] so you are ready to apply these rules whenever a scene presents itself. Developing your "photographic eye" takes time, so keep practicing these compositions until they become second nature.
