# Lighting Conditions

Light is the most fundamental element in any photograph. Understanding how it interacts with the urban landscape—bouncing off glass, reflecting on wet pavement, or creating harsh shadows—is critical to successful street photography.

## Types of Light
* **Golden Hour:** Occurs shortly after sunrise or before sunset. It provides soft, warm light that is perfect for candid portraits.
* **Blue Hour:** The period just after sunset. This creates moody, cool-toned shots and works well with neon city lights.
* **Harsh Midday Sun:** This creates high-contrast, dramatic black and white photos. It is challenging but can result in very graphic compositions.

> "The sun is the only light source that matters, but it is not always your friend."

Mastering how to read the light helps you decide when to use your [[essential-gear]] and informs your choice of [[camera-basics]]. If you are shooting in low-light urban alleys, remember to adjust your settings before you start framing your shots using the principles found in [[composition-rules]]. Being able to anticipate how light will change as you move through different streets is a skill that separates amateurs from professionals.
